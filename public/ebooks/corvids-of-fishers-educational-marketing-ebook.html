Sample Activities: K-2

Early Elementary Focus: Basic Animal Needs & Habitats

üè† Kindergarten: Corvid Family Tree

Episode Connection: "The Intelligence Network"

Description: Students sort corvid pictures by observable characteristics and create simple family trees, connecting to human family structures.

Materials: Corvid picture cards, large paper, glue sticks, markers

Assessment: Students identify one corvid species and explain identifying characteristics

Cross-Curricular: Math (counting/sorting), ELA (vocabulary), Social Studies (family structures)

üéµ Grade 1: Blue Jay Mimic Game

Episode Connection: "The Imitator"

Description: Students distinguish between Blue Jay calls and actual hawk calls, learning about communication strategies.

Materials: Audio recordings, speaker, sound identification cards

Assessment: Students explain why Blue Jays mimic predator sounds

Cross-Curricular: Math (patterns), ELA (listening skills), Social Studies (communication methods)

üó∫Ô∏è Grade 2: Fishers Memory Map

Episode Connection: "The Generational Memory"

Description: Create neighborhood maps showing important locations like crow families do, including interviews with family members.

Materials: Local maps, colored pencils, interview forms, clipboards

Assessment: Students explain how memory helps animals and humans survive

Cross-Curricular: Math (measurement), ELA (interview skills), Social Studies (community changes)

Sample Activities: 3-5

Upper Elementary Focus: Environmental Impact & Ecosystem Analysis

üß™ Grade 3: Corvid Intelligence Lab

Episode Connection: "The Tool Users"

Description: Students design problem-solving tests using classroom materials, comparing their strategies to corvid methods.

Materials: Puzzle pieces, containers, various tools, timer, recording sheets

Assessment: Students document problem-solving strategies and compare to corvid methods

Cross-Curricular: Math (measurement/timing), ELA (scientific method), Social Studies (innovation)

üèòÔ∏è Grade 4: Urban Planning Project

Episode Connection: "The Future Flock"

Description: Design corvid-friendly modifications to Fishers subdivisions, presenting proposals with scientific justification.

Materials: Planning maps, design tools, presentation materials, research resources

Assessment: Students present proposals with scientific justification

Cross-Curricular: Math (scale/proportion), ELA (research/presentations), Social Studies (civic engagement)

üåä Grade 5: Watershed Analysis

Episode Connection: All episodes

Description: Map energy flow through White River ecosystems, including corvid roles as seed dispersers and scavengers.

Materials: Ecosystem diagrams, colored arrows, research materials, poster board

Assessment: Students explain ecosystem impacts if corvids disappeared

Cross-Curricular: Math (data analysis), ELA (technical writing), Social Studies (environmental policy)
